Dataset

The LOAF dataset is overhead fisheye dataset using the 360° perspective capabilities of fisheye camera. It comprises 43,542 high-resolution images with 457K radius align annotations of person body detection and localization.
Features

Distinguished from most other datasets, LOAF adopts a vertical downward overhead approach using fisheye cameras, fixed at a height of 2.5 to 4 meters, providing a 360-degree panoramic view.
Collection

we capture 110 fisheye image sequences in 80 realistic scenarios as the raw data pool. The recorded sequences span 14 hrs; the image resolution is 2952×2952 pixels, and the frame rate is 10∼20 fps. Eventually, 43,542 images, sam pled at 1 fps, are collected to construct our LOAF dataset.

Marking

Person Detection We instead label each person through a radius-aligned rectangular box. fits well radially- oriented human bodies presented in fisheye images; and better corresponds to the actual position of human on the image plane, facilitating physical localization. around 458K human box annotations are obtained. High-quality annotation is ensured via a rigorous quality check, conducted by highly skilled reviewers.

Person Localization For each annotated box, we can easily infer the standing position of the person in the image by utilizing the radius-align feature, and then use the mapping relationship to project each person into real-world space to obtain their location information.

Statistics

LOAF distinguishes itself from three aspects: Large-scale: LOAF has 43,542 fisheye images with more than 457K person boxes. Moreover, LOAF data are captured by an advanced fisheye camera, which is capable of covering a larger area (200∼300 m2) with higher pedestrian density (2∼65 persons per scene, 10.5 in average). This makes LOAF the largest overhead fisheye dataset in terms of the total number of pedestrian and scene categories.
